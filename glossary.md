# Glossary of Terms  
### *(For the BulkLoad Logic Series: Parts 1–3 + Narratives)*

This glossary collects all key terms used across the narratives, essays, and explanations.  
It is meant to serve as a reference for readers navigating the layered logic systems described in the project.

---

# 1. Logic Systems

## **Causal Logic (CL)**
The logic of **dead matter**, physics, and linear cause → effect.  
Used for:
- material consequences  
- legal definitions  
- engineering  
- survival  

CL collapses meaning into **one layer**: physical mechanism.

---

## **Goal‑Based Logic (GBL)**
The logic of **living systems**, direction, coherence, and purpose.  
Used for:
- motivation  
- honour  
- belonging  
- long‑term stability  

GBL statements are **positive‑only**: they affirm direction rather than describe mechanisms.

---

## **Material Logic**
A cultural form of CL.  
Focuses on:
- obstacles  
- survival  
- immediate consequences  
- “not dying”  

Often used by groups under pressure or scarcity.

---

## **Spiritual Logic**
A cultural form of GBL.  
Focuses on:
- purpose  
- honour  
- belonging  
- “living well”  

Often used by groups with stable survival conditions.

---

# 2. Social & Symbolic Concepts

## **Coherence**
The degree to which a group or system maintains:
- shared direction  
- shared purpose  
- stable identity  

Loss of coherence = symbolic death.

---

## **Belonging**
A GBL concept describing:
- emotional inclusion  
- motivational alignment  
- shared identity  

Belonging is a stabilizing force in living systems.

---

## **Directionality**
The “vector” of a system’s purpose.  
In GBL, direction is more important than immediate success.

---

## **Honour**
A spiritual/GBL term describing:
- integrity  
- motivation  
- alignment with group purpose  

Not reducible to material consequences.

---

## **Symbolic Accusation**
A GBL accusation that signals:
- misalignment  
- weakening of coherence  
- threat to future stability  

Not a claim of physical wrongdoing.

---

## **Materialization of Symbol**
When a symbolic accusation becomes materially true because:
- the accused rejects symbolic meaning  
- the accused reacts materially  
- the reaction disrupts the system  

A self‑fulfilling prophecy.

---

# 3. Behavioural Archetypes

## **Linear Thinker**
A person who:
- uses one logic only  
- collapses multi‑layered meaning  
- interprets metaphors literally  
- responds materially to symbolic statements  

Creates paradoxes and self‑fulfilling crimes.

---

## **Logic Tourist**
Someone who enters a symbolic/GBL environment but listens with CL.  
They:
- misinterpret  
- escalate  
- accuse  
- disrupt  

Not malicious — simply misaligned.

---

## **Logic Colonizer**
Someone who insists:
- their logic is the only valid one  
- all statements must be translated into their logic  
- other logics are “irrational”  

A form of interpretive domination.

---

# 4. Eastern & Philosophical Concepts

## **Wu Wei**
From Taoism:  
“Non‑forcing”, “effortless action”, “acting without overacting”.

In this project:
- prevents escalation  
- avoids symbolic → material collapse  
- maintains coherence  
- allows peaceful conditioning  

---

## **Phantom Response**
A game‑theoretic pattern where:
- a person responds to a move that was never made  
- the response creates a new conflict  
- the response becomes the offense  

Central to the second narrative.

---

## **Self‑Fulfilling Prophecy**
A symbolic statement that becomes true because of:
- misinterpretation  
- overreaction  
- defensive behaviour  

A core mechanism in both narratives.

---

# 5. Systems & Scientific Terms

## **Teleonomy**
Goal‑directed behaviour emerging from biological systems.  
Not mystical — evolutionary.

---

## **Autopoiesis**
Self‑maintaining systems (Maturana & Varela).  
Life maintains itself through circular processes.

---

## **Predictive Processing**
Neuroscience model where the brain minimizes prediction error.  
A form of GBL in biological terms.

---

## **Complexity & Emergence**
Systems that produce:
- direction  
- order  
- coherence  
from simple parts.

---

## **Coherence Death**
When a system loses:
- direction  
- belonging  
- purpose  

Equivalent to “spiritual death” in the narratives.

---

# 6. Narrative‑Specific Terms

## **Killing the Future**
A GBL accusation meaning:
- breaking coherence  
- dissolving belonging  
- weakening direction  

Not physical harm.

---

## **Magic Materializes**
When symbolic meaning becomes material because:
- someone insists on literal interpretation  
- someone reacts materially  
- the system shifts logic  

A metaphor for logic collision.

---

## **The Archive / The City of Layers**
Narrative settings representing:
- multi‑logic environments  
- symbolic spaces  
- coherence‑based communities  

---

# End of Glossary
